The Carpyz wheel

Brings together a Turbo, a Propeller and a circular Reactor.

The Carpyz wheel is a propeler

The air sucked in by the rotation of the Carpyz Wheel passes through it like a propeller.
The rotation generates a forward thrust and traction.

Like a turbine, the Carpyz Wheel circulates air through its hollow blades

The rotation causes the air to enter the front in the centre in a circular chamber.
From this chamber, hollow blades emerge and open into a peripheral circular chamber with slots surrounding the Carpyz Wheel.Â 
The air circulates by centrifugal force through these blades to the peripheral chamber.

The Carpyz Wheel also circulates a fluid in its hollow blades.

An energetic fluid is injected from behind.
The fluid also flows through the hollow blades to the peripheral chamber.

The Carpyz Wheel acts as a double reactor

The propeller drives the wheel forward.
The slots at the rear project the air and energy fluid into the surrounding environment as it rotates and accentuate the rotation by this double reaction.
The Carpyz Wheel thus projects its full thrust potential.

The Carpyz turbine

A more efficient turbine to generate power

The Carpyz turbine is based on the principle of the Pelton turbine.

The fluid enters at the front in a fixed duct as in a windsock.
The duct is surrounded by blades that turn and rotate the fluid.

The Carbyz Turbine is a strong power generator.

At the outlet of the duct the fluid hits spoon-shaped blades located all around a wheel.
The rotation of the wheel provides mechanical, hydraulic and electrical energy.
